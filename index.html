About CryptoSteg

What is Steganography?

Steganography is the practice of concealing information within seemingly ordinary files or messages. Unlike encryption, which makes data unreadable but detectable, steganography hides the very existence of the secret data, making it invisible to the casual observer.

How CryptoSteg Works

CryptoSteg combines steganography with encryption for maximum security:

  1. LSB (Least Significant Bit) Steganography - CryptoSteg hides data by manipulating the least significant bits of pixels in an image, making changes imperceptible to the human eye.
  2. Encryption - Before hiding, your data can be encrypted using various algorithms like AES, making the hidden information unreadable without the correct key.

Security Features

  • Client-Side Processing - All operations happen directly in your browser; no data is transmitted to any server.
  • Multiple Encryption Algorithms - Choose from AES, DES, Triple DES, Rabbit, RC4, or simple Caesar ciphers.
  • Password Protection - Add a password to encrypt your hidden data for extra security.

Usage Notes

  • For best results, use PNG images as they provide lossless compression, preserving the hidden data.
  • Remember your encryption password; there's no way to recover data without it!
  • The amount of data you can hide depends on the image dimensions. Larger images can hide more data.
